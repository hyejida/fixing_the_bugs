ICBC Usability Case Study

Project Details

Role: UX/UI Designer, Web Development

Tools Used: Figma, Adobe Illustrator

Team: Michelle Apostol, Carissa So, Hyeji Kang

Timeline: 3 Months

Describe a test plan for conducting a usability test for ICBC.com. The goals of usability testing include establishing a baseline of user performance, establishing and validating user performance measures, and identifying potential design concerns to be addressed in order to improve the efficiency, productivity, and end-user satisfaction.

The Objectives

To determine design inconsistencies and usability problem areas within the user interface and content areas. Potential sources of error may include:

Navigation Errors

Failure to locate functions, excessive keystrokes to complete a function, failure to follow recommended screen flow.

Presentation Errors

Failure to locate and properly act upon desired information in screens, selection errors due to labeling ambiguities.

Control Usage Problems

improper toolbar or entry field usage. Exercise the web site under controlled test conditions with representative users.

Archetypes

Based on our research we developed the following list of archetypes who would use ICBC's website. By creating this list of archetypes, it helps us (the evaluators) to understand who our typical users are and develop a stronger emotional connection with the user. By knowing our users, we have a better idea of how they will interact with ICBC’s services, navigation and understand their goals and model a typical journey on the site.

  • Teenagers
  • New Drivers
  • Busy Parents
  • Non-Residents of BC
  • Retirees

User Persona

We recruited 3 participants from out collective pool of friends and family with an effort to select who would fit as a typical user with pre-test questions informing us on their demographics, how often they visit ICBC.com and their familiarity with the site in general.

Procedure

Pre-test Questionnaire

To ensure our participants fit our ideal user for ICBC based on the archetypes we listed, the participants were guided though a series of questions on their demographics and past experience, if any, with ICBC's website. These questions were a combination of multiple choice and written.

Usability Test Questions

In this section of the test, we conducted three types of tests to understand what the user can recall, where the user would click to accomplish a task and their preferences in the design of the navigation interface on ICBC.com.

Measurement & Evaluation

Task Completion Rates

This table summarizes the completion rate and the average time it took the participants to complete each task in the Think Out Loud testing. Tasks completed within 3.5min were counted as a successful completion. Given this criteria, we found that each participant was only able to complete 2 of the 3 given tasks on time.

System Usability Scale(SUS)

For each "Think Out Loud" task, an "impact" number between 1-5 is determined. If the task is very important then a high number is chosen.1 being not important, and 5 being very important (or high impact).

Heuristic Evaluation

A heuristic analysis is conducted to evaluate the user interface design by systematically evaluating its usability concerns. As a small set of evaluators, we took a close look at ICBC.com using an expertly crafted pre-built questionnaire to give us insights on the most pressing concerns for our UX/UI work.

Proposed changes

1. Adding small icons

users chose to use the global navigation over the card navigation. This is because card navigation depended on the user screen size. We put small six icons for the services that ICBC users look for frequently further up on the homepage. Using icons is good to present blocks of information visually and draw attention.

2. Having the ICBC handbook cover

By having the ICBC handbook cover on the page, it can give the page a focus point. Additionally, the button can help users navigate the task of downloading the handbook easily.
